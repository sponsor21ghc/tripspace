Originally, I was thinking that a UBOX would fit into this truck box smoothly, and transport on the interstate system. The problem, it turns out, is that the UBOX unit is 8 feet deep by 5 feet wide, and then 8 feet tall. Transporting 3 if these UBOX units will work somewhat with a pull behind trailer with the 2 UBOX units settled into it in their standardised fashion (it takes someone with UBOX experience to grasp this). The overall problem though, is the fact that the UBOX is 5 feet wide, and the pickup bed can only accomodate 4 foot wide stuff. It still may work however.

The truck bed sits 3 feet off of the ground, and the UBOX would have to stick way up into the air, but still not more than 13 feet 6 inches. It may be doable, but quite awkward.

The "workable" width of the bed is only 4 feet (between the wheel wells), but the UBOX is 5 feet wide. This still may work because the top of said UBOX would be approx 12 to 13 feet off of ground (which leaves 6 inches or better of space above -- to fit the 13 foot 6 inch DOT specification).
